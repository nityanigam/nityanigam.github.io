Nitya Nigam

RESEARCH

Simulating the effects of dust on multiwavelength emission from tidal disruption events (TDEs)

Mentors: Prof. Vikram Ravi and Jean Somarwal,  Caltech Department of Astronomy​ | Jun 2024 — present

  • Modeling the transmission of TDE emission from across the EM spectrum through SMBH-surrounding gas and dust, with the aim of better understanding observed TDE SED sand how to relate TDE lightcurves at different wavebands
  • Using SKIRT code to model radiative transfer through various dust geometries and assumed properties

Investigating the ability of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) to generate neutron star magnetic fields

Mentors: Prof. Andrei Beloborodov and Dr. Valentin Skoutnev, Columbia University Department of Physics | May 2023 — present

  • Studying the CME's capability to generate large-scale magnetic fields in both linear and non-linear neutron star environments
  • Using both analytical and numerical approaches (Dedalus, a numerical PDE solver in Python)
  • Presented posters at the Simons Collaboration on Extreme Electrodynamics of Compact Sources (SCEECS) meeting and Columbia University's AstroFest

Polarization design considerations for the Next Generation Event Horizon Telescope (ngEHT)

Mentor: Dr. Lindy Blackbur​n, Harvard & Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics​ | May 2022 — Apr 2024

  • Used Fisher analysis to constrain uncertainties on pairs of parameters in a single-baseline model of ngEHT imaging pipeline 
  • Developed an HMC forward model in Python to find covariances between parameter pairs in the 3-baseline case, informing design decisions for the ngEHT including use of mixed-basis measurements and rotation between measurements
  • Presented work at AAS 241st Meeting in January 2023 and at Harvard Black Hole Initiative Group Meetings in July 2022 and January 2023

Emission modeling in the EHT-ngEHT age

Mentor: Dr. Richard Anantua, Harvard & Smithsonian Center for Astrophysics​; University of Texas San Antonio | Nov — Dec 2022

  • Produced temperature models and intensity maps for emission models of jet/accretion flow/black hole (JAB) systems
  • Helped address concerns raised in referee report upon submission to journal
  • Work was published in  Galaxies vol. 11  (2023)
